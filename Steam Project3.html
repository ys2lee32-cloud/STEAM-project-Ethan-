Rolling time on a wooden ramp

hypothesis: If the surface of a ball is smoother and harder, then it will roll faster down a ramp because it experiences less friction with the wooden surface.

In contrast, balls with rougher, softer, or more deformable surfaces (such as clay or rubber/slime) will roll more slowly because they create greater friction and may slightly stick or deform against the ramp.

According to Newton's Second Law of Motion, the acceleration of an object depends on the net force acting on it.

Since mass and gravity remain constant in this experiment, friction is the main variable affecting motion.

Higher friction reduces acceleration, increasing the time it takes for the ball to reach the bottom of the ramp.

Therefore, it is predicted that harder and smoother materials such as urethane or metal will roll the fastest, while softer and rougher materials such as clay or rubber (slime ball) will roll the slowest.
